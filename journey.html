My Life Journey

I am not one to tell long stories but on March 25th, 1996, I was born in Visalia, CA and raised in Tulare, CA.

I also have autism spectrum disorder which in a nutshell means that my brain processes things and thinks divergently than most people would.

I however have over the years used my autism to my advantage to get me ahead of my life.

And I have had a lot of highs and lows but have grown stronger over the years. Below is the story of how I became a graphic designer.



Tulare Raised

I was born in Visalia, California and grew up in Tulare, California for my entire life. I also grew up while being on the autism spectrum.

While everyone in my hometown often worked everyday monotonously, I would often look at logos, magazine covers, billboards, and packaging labels everywhere I went as I would always notice if designs changed.

I guess you could say that was where my journey as a graphic designer began.



Soaring High at Mission Oak

I was a very studious student growing up as I would often get good grades, but I also had a difficult time making and keeping friends due to my autism.

However, when I got to Mission Oak High School, I was accepted with open arms and opportunities started opening up for me.

I helped redesign the varsity football helmets, made posters for numerous sporting events, and even gave good ideas for ASB to promote school spirit. I graduated Mission Oak in 2015 as a co-valedictorian and was even named most spirited for my graduating class.

I had high honors in all subjects. High school was like a home away from home as it allowed me to blossom and be free as an autistic individual and I consider high school to be the best years of my life.



Giant Leaps at COS

I started COS in 2015 and realized right away that college was not like high school as everyone was less sociable and more career focused.

I had enrolled as a part-time student as I did not want to stress myself out too much and wanted to go slow and steady.

In 2019 however, I was getting burned out and I decided to take a break for a year to get my mind cleared up and had planned to return in the Fall of 2020 to finish my degree...

...And then the COVID-19 Pandemic came.

During the pandemic, I was stuck at home wondering if there was even a future for me.
I honestly was at the lowest point in my life.

But then in 2022, we were all finally allowed to do normal activities and I decided to finish what I started at COS and complete my degree. This time as a full-time student.

And now, I am going to be graduating with an Associate of Science in Graphic Design in May.



Sliding into Home with the Rawhide

My overall goal is to find employment, so I decided to search for internships during my second stint at COS and then the opportunity of a lifetime came.

I was able to land a Marketing/Graphic Design Internship with the Visalia Rawhide. I have been an avid fan of baseball as it was baseball that allowed me to be able to go out to social places as a young child.

And my first baseball game coincidentally was a Visalia Rawhide game back in the days when they were called the Visalia Oaks. that one game led me to watch Fresno Grizzlies games, and later Los Angeles Dodger games.

And to be able to intern with the team that made me love baseball and to use my talents to promote their brand and identity is honestly a dream come true. I almost feel like I am in some Cinderella story where I am having all my dreams coming true. It really shows how when you work hard, your dreams do indeed come true.
